Warning Tags

The Enderwalk Archives have chosen, for legal and other reasons, to mandate that users either warn for—or explicitly choose not to warn for—a short list of common warnings. We understand that creators may wish to not warn for some of these things, or to warn for additional content, and have provided options for them to do so within this framework.

Abuse:
This is for explicit representations of abuse in any form. This includes, but is not limited to: Physical, emotional and verbal abuse.
Alcohol & Drug Use:
Use of drugs or alcohol in a work.
Bigotry & Discrimination:
This is for stories that have themes of characters facing bigotry. This is not an excuse to post bigoted material.
Choose Not To Use Archive Warnings:
Use this if you don't want to warn for anything. You may also choose this option if you don't know what you should warn for; if you don't like warning for certain topics or warnings in general; if you want to avoid some spoilers, but not others; etc.
Graphic Depictions Of Violence:
This is for gory, graphic, explicitly described violence. Exactly where to draw the line is your call.
Major Character Death:
Please use your best judgment about who counts as a major character.
No Archive Warnings Apply:
Use this if the Archive warnings don't apply to your content. (In other words, if it contains no graphic depictions of violence, major character death, rape/non-con, or underage sexual activity.)
Self-Harm:
This is for explicit representations of both physical and mental self-harm.
Suicide:
This is for explicit representations of suicide or attempted suicide.
Torture:
This is for explicit representations of torture in any form. This includes, but is not limited to: Physical, emotional and psychological torture.
Unreality & Derealization:
This is for content that may trigger psychosis or derealization, such as content about the world being a simulation or characters (in a non reader insert context) speaking directly to the reader. Use your best judgement.

You can also use the "Additional Tags" field to give other or more detailed warnings. Our policies regarding warnings can be found in the Terms of Service and Terms of Service FAQ.
